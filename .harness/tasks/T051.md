## Overview
On the individual plant detail screen the schedule card's two inline glyphs balloon to fill the lower half of the screen (a giant dark-green droplet and an enormous tan pencil), crushing the real content to a sliver. Root cause: these are asset-catalog TEMPLATE images (ChromeIcon.image = Image(name).renderingMode(.template), from Sources/DesignSystem/SproutIcon.swift) sized only with .font(.system(size:)) and no .resizable()/.frame() — and .font does NOT size a non-SF-Symbol template image, so with no enclosing fixed frame they render unbounded.

## Do
In Sources/Views/PlantDetailView.swift scheduleCard, fix the two unbounded glyphs: (1) the droplet at ~lines 154-155 — `ChromeIcon.droplet.image` currently `.font(.system(size: 14, weight: .semibold))` — and (2) the pencil at ~lines 171-172 — `ChromeIcon.pencil.image` currently `.font(.system(size: 14))`. Replace the `.font(.system(size:...))` sizing on each with the resizable pattern used elsewhere in the app (e.g. HomeView: `.resizable().aspectRatio(contentMode: .fit).frame(width: 14, height: 14)`), keeping each glyph's existing `.foregroundStyle(...)` (droplet = dueColor, pencil = SproutTheme.taupe) and its surrounding layout. The droplet sits inside a ZStack with a `Circle().frame(34)`; size the droplet to ~14pt so it sits inside that circle rather than outgrowing it. Do NOT change any glyph anywhere else — audit finding: the other `.image + .font(.system)` sites (PlantToken.swift:64, RhythmBand.swift:75, IconPickerView.swift:162, LaunchSplashView.swift:35) each wrap the glyph in a ZStack with an outer `.frame(...)` that already bounds it, so they render fine and are out of scope here.
